# Hackathon Overview

## Event

**Robotics × AI Hackathon — Berlin (first edition)**
The first Berlin "Robotics × Agentic coding" Hackathon. A weekend of building at the intersection of
robotics and AI agents. Open to robotics engineers, software devs, designers, and founders.

> Mission (organizers' words): *bridge the gaps between companies, the public, investors, and builders
> in Robotics and AI, and demonstrate the European ecosystem's talent in mixing these two fields.*

## Theme

**Robotics × Agentic coding.** The event pairs physical robotics hardware with agentic AI / LLM
coding tools. The recurring expectation across tracks: use **agentic coding** (Claude, Codex, etc.)
and modern AI tooling to do something real with robots.

## Judging

> **No official rubric was published for the NormaCore track.** The ITQ track (bottle-cap parkour)
> has explicit scoring (caps collected, completion time, safe navigation), but that is a different
> track. **[CONFIRM ON-SITE]** whether a per-track rubric exists.

Inferred from the format (finalist pitches + working-demo deadline), judges almost certainly weight:
1. **Working demo** — does it actually run on real hardware?
2. **Innovation / technical depth** — is the approach novel and non-trivial?
3. **Business / pitch** — is there a real application and a credible company story?
4. **Use of sponsor tech** — agentic coding + the track sponsor's platform.

Our strategy is designed to score on all four (see [`03-project-vision.md`](./03-project-vision.md)).

## What "winning" looks like for us

- A live, real-hardware demo of the voice-commanded assistive arm with **visible self-correction**.
- A pitch that frames it as a fundable company (assistive → clinical/surgical expansion).
- Heavy, correct use of agentic coding (Claude/Codex as the robot's brain) — the literal track ask.
